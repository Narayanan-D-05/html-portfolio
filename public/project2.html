The Best Webtoons According to Narayanan

My Top 10 Webtoons of all-time.

  1. Lord of the Mysteries

    Set in a steampunk world filled with secret organizations and eldritch horrors, the story follows Klein Moretti, who becomes a Beyonder after stumbling into an arcane ritual. With mystery, gods, and psychological tension, this manhwa is a slow-burn masterpiece of cosmic intrigue and power ascension.

  2. Omniscient Reader’s Viewpoint

    Kim Dokja wakes up inside the world of his favorite web novel—and he's the only one who knows how the story unfolds. Using his meta-knowledge, he teams up with the protagonist to survive apocalyptic scenarios in a reality-turned-novel world. Epic, clever, and emotionally deep.

  3. Shadow Slave

    In a post-apocalyptic nightmare world, Sunny, a "slave" with a unique ability, fights for survival against terrifying creatures. This manhwa blends dark fantasy, survival horror, and psychological development with a gritty, realistic tone and a compelling underdog protagonist.

  4. Reverend Insanity

    Not your typical cultivation manhwa—this one follows Fang Yuan, a cold-blooded, calculating villain who is reborn and seeks to dominate the world using every ruthless tactic imaginable. A controversial yet fascinating journey into anti-heroism and moral ambiguity.

  5. Magic Emperor

    After being betrayed and killed, a powerful demonic cultivator reincarnates into a servant’s body. With his previous life’s knowledge, he begins climbing back to power while hiding his identity. A mix of revenge, strategy, and cultivation chaos.

  6. The Beginning After the End

    King Grey is reincarnated into a magical world as Arthur Leywin, and he gets a second chance at life. With memories of his past and a thirst for redemption, he trains to become stronger while uncovering the world's secrets. A mix of slice-of-life, fantasy, and intense magic battles.

  7. The Greatest Estate Developer

    Civil engineering meets fantasy! When a modern-day engineer is transported into a fantasy novel, he uses his knowledge to solve problems through construction, economics, and logic. Equal parts hilarious and smart, with a refreshing take on isekai tropes.

  8. Solo Leveling

    Sung Jin-Woo, once the weakest hunter, gains the ability to level up endlessly after surviving a deadly dungeon. With his new powers, he rises through the ranks, battles monstrous foes, and uncovers the truth about the system that changed his life. Peak power fantasy.

  9. Lookism

    Park Hyung Suk wakes up in a new, handsome body and discovers how differently society treats him. As he juggles his original and new appearances, he uncovers dark truths about bullying, social bias, and self-worth. A gritty mix of drama, action, and social commentary.

  10. Webtoon Character Na-Kim

    Na Kim realizes he’s just a side character in a romance webtoon—and he’s sick of it. Determined to take control of his fate, he starts rewriting the story in hilarious, chaotic, and totally unpredictable ways. A meta-comedy with clever storytelling and wild twists.
